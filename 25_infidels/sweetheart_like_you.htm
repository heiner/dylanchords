Sweetheart Like You

Words and music Bob Dylan
Released on Infidels (1983)
tabbed by Eyolf Østrem

The song sounds in E major, and it can perfectly well be played with those keys, but on a single guitar it makes for a far more comfortable and rewarding experience to play it with a capo on the 4th fret. I seem to hear one such guitar in the mix too, although the main guitar(s) are obviously uncapoed.
Other advantages with the capoed version is that the descent at the end of the verses becomes playable, and you can also easily play the Fs (some of them, not all of them) as Fmaj7s.
The Dm at the end of the bridge could be realized in a number of ways. On the album it almost converts to a Fmaj7, when the organ gradually comes to the fore. A way to combine both is to play Dm9, e.g. x53550.
