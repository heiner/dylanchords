Written by Norman Petty, Charles Hardin and Buddy Holly<br>
Performed by Bob Dylan regularly in 1999-2000 and a few times before
and after that<br>
Tabbed by Eyolf Østrem

----
Buddy Holly played this song with the basic rhythm:

<pre class="verse">
E           A         E   E
:   .   .   .     :   .   .   .
    ba      ba        ba  ba
</pre>

When The Rolling Stones covered the song, they changed the rhythm into
the “Bo Diddley Beat” and moved the chord changes:

<pre class="verse">
E                     A   E
v     v     v         v   v
:   .   .   .     :   .   .   .
</pre>

This has become the standard version of the song, which Dylan
follows. Early on he played it rather straightforwardly in the
“Stones Tradition” (see 1997 version below), but since 1999 he and
the band have worked out their own distinctive version, to which they
have remained faithful.<br>
The transcription below follows the version from Oslo, May 19, 2000,
with a side-glance at Vienna, April 30, 1999.

Capo 1st fret (sounding key G major)<br>
(this may seem awkward, but it is necessary to get the 0h2 hammer-on
on the sixth string)
